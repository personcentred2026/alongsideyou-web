About

Built by people who know the work from the inside

AlongsideYou.care exists because of a gap we kept hearing about, and eventually could not ignore. People were leaving training feeling inspired and equipped. Then going back to work and finding themselves alone with the hard moments, with no quick way to reconnect with what they had learned.

Why we built this

Training ends. Practice does not.

Over more than a decade working in personalised care workforce development, designing training programmes, writing national competency frameworks, and working directly with Care Coordinators, Social Prescribers, and Health and Wellbeing Coaches, one thing kept coming up.

People had the training. They understood person-centred practice in theory. But back in a busy PCN, between appointments, after a conversation that did not go quite right, there was nothing to turn to. No quick reference, no space to reflect, no way to reconnect with what they had learned when it actually mattered.

"We kept hearing the same thing: the training was good, but there was nothing after. We wanted to change that, to build something that sat alongside people in their day-to-day work, not just on training days."

AlongsideYou.care is the answer to that gap. Not a replacement for training, but an extension of it. A digital companion that gives practitioners quick access to what they need, space to reflect on what is happening in their practice, and a way to keep growing between formal learning opportunities.

Why this matters even more now

The shift to hybrid working and the growth of neighbourhood and community-based roles has changed how the personalised care workforce operates. Practitioners are increasingly working more independently, away from office environments and the informal support that came with them.

For a Care Coordinator working across multiple sites, a Social Prescriber embedded in a community setting, or a Health and Wellbeing Coach visiting people at home, the gap between training and day-to-day practice support has widened. AlongsideYou.care is built precisely for that context, providing the kind of accessible, flexible, on-demand support that fits how this workforce actually works today.

What we believe

A few things we hold to be true

Training days are necessary but not sufficient.

The gap between learning something and being able to use it confidently in practice is real, and it is where most workforce development investment is lost.

Reflection is not a luxury.

For practitioners doing emotionally demanding, complex work, the ability to pause and make sense of what happened is not a nice-to-have. It is what separates practice that improves from practice that stagnates. This is particularly true for those working in hybrid, remote, and neighbourhood settings where the informal support of a shared office is no longer available.

The workforce deserves better tools.

Care Coordinators, Social Prescribers, and Health and Wellbeing Coaches are doing some of the most important work in primary care. The support available to them in their day-to-day work should reflect that.

Technology should follow practice, not lead it.

AlongsideYou.care was designed around how practitioners actually work, between appointments, in five spare minutes, after a conversation that stuck with them, not around what is technically impressive.
